CS & Philosophy at Columbia. Graduating May 2026.

The work that interests me lives at the intersection of culture, image, and instinct. I take photography, paint, study film, and ran a resale shop where I reverse-engineered micro-trends before they surfaced anywhere obvious. I write cultural analysis. I design interfaces. Code gives me range. Images give me taste.

I use technology the way a director uses a camera — with intention, a point of view, and a willingness to be surprised by what comes out. Hockney and Matisse taught me that color is an argument. Film taught me to compose before I shoot.

My process: spot the signal before it becomes noise, build the thing before I pitch it, trust that a visual logic has to exist before a format does.
